Charles Lyell's visual data relationship is a case study that investigates and attempts to visualise Charles Lyell's personal relationships and their scientific impact based on data from the return address and sender, as well as some annotations from previous owners and archives of Charles Lyell's personal correspondence, both family and professional, found in the archives of the University of Edinburgh. In this resource, you can explore the scientific debates and theories that circulated in Charles Lyell's personal circle during his time and learn about many subjects.


This case study is possible thanks to the University of Edinburgh through the Cockburn Geological Museum and the Heritage Collections and the help of Dr. Gillian McCay, curator of the Cockburn Geological Museum at the University of Edinburgh, Pamela McIntyre, Strategic Projects Archivist at the Heritage Collections at the Edinburgh University Library and many students and collaborators who studied and worked with the collection and the dataset.
